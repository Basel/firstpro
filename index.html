About me

I'm currently second year ICT student, majoring Business IT & Management at the Hanze University of Applied Science in Groningen, the Netherlands. Previously, I studied English Translation in Homs University for three years, in 2009 I started my online translation business (Victoria LTS) and developed my skills as a video subtitler and worked as freelance subtitler for 5 years.

I’m originally from Syria and was born in 1989, I live now in Groningen, I speak Arabic (Native), English, Dutch and Esperanto; and due to the fact that my subtitling job allowed me to work on different languages, I’m very familiar to confidently work on different languages such as: Greek, Polish and Spanish.

Skills & Projects

Even though subtitling was my career for years, I'm eager these days to build and develop my ICT skills and be prepared for my soon-future career as a project manager or business architect. Studying at Hanze is giving me the necessary skills to land in a career confidently.

Some of the project and the skills I have learnt/done are:
- Web development using HTML/CSS/PHP/MYSQL.
- Data analysis and data visualization via Tableau.
- Setting up a business case in PRINCE2 framework.
- Enterprise architecture using TOGAF framework.

Subtitling Services

My work was outsourced to several companies such as: Netflix and SONY, I also worked for different individual clients such as: EasyDaf, GUT protocol, Irish media, Health & Performance by Gavin Heward and have in my account more than 30.000 minutes subtitled in 5 languages (English, Arabic, Dutch, German and Spanish). Beside subtitling services, I also trained nearly 100 Arabic-speaking students on subtitling via ATIDA.

My subtitling services cover all subtitling aspects, starting from transcribing, translation, timecoding, proofreading and embedding. I also offer customized subtitle service such as karaoke, captioning and SLS (Same-language subtitle). High quality is always guaranteed.

Podcast

I’m not a podcast producer nor appeared in any podcast (Except the two records I once made in Arabic giving tips on how to learn English and another one where I’m explaining the Dutch education system for Arabic speakers), but people who know me personally, likely know that I’m addicted to listen to podcasts, I use it as a way to feed my curiosity, expand my knowledge and learn more about science, human psychology and world; I use podcast as an alternative to books. And since I’m often asked to recommend some shows, I decided to add a page to my own website showing my top podcasts, here we are!


- Radiolab: A radio show and podcast presenting stories and science into sound and music-rich documentaries.
- Hidden Brain: The Hidden Brain helps curious people understand the world – and themselves. Using science and storytelling, Hidden Brain's host Shankar Vedantam reveals the unconscious patterns that drive human behavior, the biases that shape our choices, and the triggers that direct the course of our relationships.
- Death, sex and money:: A podcast hosted by Anna Sale about the big questions and hard choices that are often left out of polite conversation
- Invisibilia Unseeable forces control human behavior and shape our ideas, beliefs, and assumptions. Invisibilia—Latin for invisible things—fuses narrative storytelling with science that will make you see your own life differently.
- If I Were You Show Jake Hurwitz and Amir Blumenfeld offer up advice in areas they're qualified to talk about. Also in areas they're not qualified to talk about! This shows has nothing to do with science!
- In mijn hoofd(Dutch): Anke Van Meer tries to learn the sounds of the concept we have in our heads and can't see it like languages and love.
- Varsovia Vento (Esperanto)"Warsaw Wind" or "Varsovia Vento" In Esperanto is monthly podcast recorded in Poland by Irek (Mainly), Agnieszka (Rudzia), Sylwia and sometimes they have more guests, the podcast talks about events, meetups, new songs and interviews with Esperantists from all over the world.
